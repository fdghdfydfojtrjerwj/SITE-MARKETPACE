Also, having a built-in SD card slot and a full-sized HDMI port again means I can finally leave my annoying dongles at home.

Studio-grade sound in a portable chassis. I use this laptop in my studio for heavy music production in Logic Pro. Ethan J.

Heavy and bulky, but the performance jump is real. I upgraded from a MacBook Air, and the difference is massive. Everything feels smoother because of the 120Hz screen, and the ports are incredibly useful. However, be prepared for the weight. The Pro 14 is noticeably thicker and heavier in a backpack compared to the Air line. If you don't actually need the raw rendering power, the Air might still be the better option for pure travel.

Elijah W.

Powerful hardware with almost no games to play. I bought this hoping the raw GPU power would let me do some gaming on the side. While the hardware is technically capable of running heavy graphics, the macOS gaming ecosystem is still terrible. Most modern AAA games are completely unavailable, and running them through translation layers is a buggy mess. It is an incredible tool for spreadsheets and video editing, but a terrible choice for entertainment.

Luka T.

Great machine, but external monitor limitations are frustrating. The laptop itself is incredibly fast, and the battery life easily gets me through 12 hours of office work. However, if you buy the base processor option, Apple still limits you on how many external monitors you can natively plug in. For a machine with Pro in the name, having to buy the more expensive chip versions just to connect my multi-monitor desk setup is a frustrating cash grab.

Emma S.

Incredible hardware, but Apple's upgrade pricing is robbery. There is no denying that the performance, the trackpad, and the screen are top-tier. But Apple’s pricing structure is incredibly frustrating. Since everything is soldered to the motherboard, you cannot upgrade the storage or RAM later. If you want to configure it with enough specs to future-proof it for the next 5 years, the price sky-rockets into an absurd territory.

Andrey Y.

Terrible reliability and expensive repair costs. I took great care of this laptop, but just over a year into owning it, thin lines started appearing across the screen. Out of nowhere, the display backlight failed completely. Since it was just past the standard 1-year warranty, Apple customer service quoted me an astronomical price to replace the entire top assembly. For a premium machine that costs this much, having the screen fail so quickly is completely unacceptable.
